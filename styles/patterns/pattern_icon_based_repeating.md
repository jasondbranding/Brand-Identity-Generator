## For LOGOS:

### 1. Form Language
- **Dominant Shapes:** Primary use of fundamental geometric shapes including perfect circles, squares, rectangles, triangles (especially right-angled and equilateral), diamonds, and arcs.
- **Geometry Rules:** Strict adherence to Euclidean geometry. Forms are constructed with precise angles (predominantly 0, 45, 90 degrees) and curves. Emphasize orthogonal and radial symmetry.
- **Construction Principles:** Logos are built upon modular assembly, concentric arrangements, or radial arrangements. Elements are precisely interlocked, aligned, or spaced. Often derived from an underlying implied grid system.

### 2. Style Constraints
- **Rendering Style:** Strictly 2D, flat vector graphic appearance. Utilizes solid color fills exclusively. No gradients, textures, photographic elements, or simulations of three-dimensional depth (e.g., shadows, highlights, bevels).
- **Fill vs Outline:** Predominantly solid filled shapes. When outlines are used, they must be uniform in weight and function as integral structural components, not mere decorative strokes.
- **Complexity Level:** Minimalist and iconic. Composed of a limited number of distinct geometric elements that combine to form a clear, recognizable symbol.

### 3. Technical Specs
- **Stroke Weights:** If outlines are employed, they must be consistent and substantial, typically within a range of 1/8 to 1/16 of the smallest significant element's dimension. Maintain uniform stroke weight across all outlined elements.
- **Proportions:** All elements within the logo adhere to perfect geometric ratios (e.g., 1:1, 1:2, 1:sqrt(2)) or are based on a consistent underlying grid unit. Proportional relationships between elements are clear and intentional.
- **Spacing:** Negative space is a fundamental design element. Spacing between individual elements is consistent and deliberate, often equal to or a multiple of the stroke weight or a base grid unit.
- **Scalability:** Logos must be designed for infinite scalability, maintaining crispness and legibility at all display sizes without pixelation or loss of detail.

### 4. Composition
- **Centering:** The primary logo mark must be perfectly center-aligned within its implied bounding box.
- **Padding:** A generous clear space (minimum padding) around the logo is essential, typically equal to or greater than 25% of the logo's width or height (whichever is smaller) on all sides. This clear space should contain no other elements.
- **Figure-Ground Ratio:** High contrast is maintained between the logo's foreground elements and its background. The distinction between positive and negative space must be unambiguous.

### 5. Avoid (Logos)
- Organic, freeform, abstract expressionist, or hand-drawn shapes.
- Complex illustrations, photographic elements, or intricate pictograms.
- Gradients, drop shadows, inner shadows, bevels, embossing, reflections, or other dimensional effects.
- Distorted, skewed, or imperfect geometric shapes (e.g., non-perfect circles, misaligned lines).
- Thin, delicate, inconsistent, or tapered stroke weights.
- Overly intricate details that hinder scalability or legibility at small sizes.
- Asymmetrical or unbalanced compositions that lack visual harmony.
- Cluttered designs with insufficient negative space.
